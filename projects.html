Annual Survey of Industries (ASI)

An R package that builds a long-format dataset from India’s Annual Survey of Industries (ASI) and stores it in a local DuckDB database.

The package provides helpers to download and organise multiple ASI rounds, query tables on the fly with dplyr, and construct state × industry × year panels for further analysis.

Example use cases include computing indicators such as average manufacturing days by rural/urban status, and mapping state-level industrial activity with sf and ggplot2.

Annual Survey of Unincorporated Sector Enterprises (ASUSE)

An R package for working with the Annual Survey of Unincorporated Sector Enterprises (ASUSE) for India, exposed as a long-format database via DuckDB.

The package downloads raw ASUSE files, builds a local database, and lets users query large tables directly with dplyr (e.g. rural–urban shares of enterprises, sectoral distributions, and regional aggregates).

It is designed for scalable exploration of the unincorporated sector, including tasks like computing the proportion of rural enterprises by NSS-region and visualising patterns on maps using sf and ggplot2.

GitHub Repository

Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS)

An R package that constructs a long-format dataset from the Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) for India and serves it via a local DuckDB database.

It streamlines downloading and organising PLFS household and person files, and enables fast queries with dplyr for tasks such as tracking the composition of households by social group or examining changes across survey rounds.

The package is geared towards labour market research, including applications like mapping mean household expenditure by state with sf and ggplot2, despite fragmented documentation and inconsistent codes.

GitHub Repository

BharatViz

An interactive Shiny dashboard that brings together industrial clusters (from ASI/ASUSE), labour market inequality and migration (PLFS), and socio-economic deprivation (SECC/NFHS/Census) in a single interface.

Users can explore heatmaps, graphs, and bivariate maps; overlay datasets; and probe questions such as: “Do industrial clusters coincide with deprived districts?” or “Which industries should a district diversify into next?”

The dashboard includes trajectory classification, diversification suggestions, and night-lights-based validation of emerging clusters.
